Pilot Exams in India

The Flight Training Program is a must for converting to DGCA CPL. It equips pilots with the skills and knowledge to fly safely and smartly. The following hours are required in the last 6 months for a pilot to be eligible to convert to DGCA CPL

  1. The Written Exam is conducted by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA). To be eligible, you must have completed 10+2 with Physics and Maths as mandatory subjects.
  2. Medical Examination must be done by Authorized Medical Examiners and a Class 1 Medical Certificate is compulsory.
  3. Pilot Aptitude Test criteria vary from airline to airline. These tests assess your skills, knowledge, and suitability for the role.

Pilot Licensing in India

Pursuing a pilot license in India is a complex yet thrilling process. It involves rigorous training, intense exams, and practical evaluations. Those wanting to become pilots must meet the criteria set by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA). This is the regulatory body for civil aviation in India. To obtain a pilot license in India, first you must complete the flying hours as prescribed by DGCA. To do this, enrol in a recognized flying school or institute. Here, aspiring pilots learn theoretical and practical training. Subjects include aircraft systems, navigation, meteorology, aviation regulations, and flight operations. After training, written exams are held by DGCA. These assess an understanding of aviation and air safety. After that, practical examinations take place. Candidates fly solo and check flights with examiners designated by DGCA. On top of all this, aspiring pilots must meet medical fitness standards set by DGCA.
