  1. Step 1

    Meet the Eligibility Criteria

    Age: 17+, 10+2 completion with â‰¥50% in physics and maths, valid DGCA Class 1 or 2 medical certificate.

    Step 2

    Choose Flying School

    Research approved DGCA flying schools in India, compare facilities, course duration, and fees for informed decision.

    Step 3

    Enrol in a Pilot Training Course

    Enroll in pilot training course at selected school, covering ground, flight, and simulator training; duration varies by license type.

    Step 4

    Complete Flight Hours and Exams

    For a commercial pilot license, fulfill 200 flight hours with 100 solo and 50 cross-country hours, passing DGCA exams. PPL requires 40-50 hours.

    Step 5

    Obtain a Commercial Pilot Licence

    Apply for a commercial pilot license from the DGCA after meeting flight hour, exam, and medical requirements, including age criteria.

    Step 6

    Build Flight Experience

    Gain experience as a co-pilot for at least 250 hours after obtaining your commercial pilot license before applying for a pilot-in-command position.
